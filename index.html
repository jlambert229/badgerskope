The system

We don't hide weak evidence.
We label it.

Every claim in BadgerSkope carries its evidence tier. Here's what they mean:

Approved / Labeled FDA or regulator-approved. This is the gold standard — real prescribing information from real drug approvals.
Major Trials Large, rigorous clinical trials in humans. The kind of data that gets drugs approved.
Early Human Small human studies or Phase 1 safety data. Promising, but not conclusive.
Animal Only Worked in mice or rats. That's interesting — but mice aren't people.
Practice-Based Used in clinics or wellness protocols. The controlled evidence? Thin at best.

Questions? Good.

Is BadgerSkope actually free?

Yes. Completely free, no account needed, no paywall. The library is open and it stays that way.

Where does the data come from?

Published clinical trials, FDA prescribing labels, PubMed, and peer-reviewed research. Every entry links to its sources. If something is based on vendor marketing, we say so explicitly.

Can I use this to decide what to take?

BadgerSkope is a research literacy tool, not a prescribing guide. It helps you understand the landscape — but any decision about your health should involve a licensed medical professional.

How do I know the evidence grades are honest?

Every grade maps to a specific type of evidence (FDA label, clinical trial, animal study, etc.). We don't inflate. If something only has mouse data, it says "Animal Only" — not "clinically proven."

Do you have a commercial relationship with any vendor?

No. BadgerSkope has zero vendor affiliations, zero affiliate links, and zero financial incentive to recommend any product.

I found an error. How do I report it?

Open an issue on our GitHub. We take accuracy seriously and will investigate every report.
