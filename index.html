FILE 02 — THE METHOD

FOUR STEPS.
NO VIBES.

Every compound on BadgerSkope goes through the same audit. The badger doesn't care who your guru is.

01
PULL THE PAPER
Every claim traces back to a primary source. PubMed, ClinicalTrials.gov, registered preprints. No “a study showed” without a DOI.
02
CHECK THE SAMPLE
Mouse vs human. N=8 vs N=2,000. Healthy vs diseased. The grade reflects who it actually worked on, not who it might.
03
STRESS THE CLAIM
Vendor copy gets line-by-line audited against the underlying data. Overstatements get flagged. Receipts attached.
04
GRADE AND FILE
Tier A through F across Evidence, Safety, and Access. Re-reviewed quarterly, or whenever new data lands.

FILE 05 — THIS WEEK'S AUDIT
FILED №0142

THE BPC-157
RECEIPT.

BY THE BADGER · 14 MIN READ · POSTED 18 APR 2026
BPC-157 has been called a “miracle peptide,” a “Wolverine drug,” and “the closest thing to actual regeneration.” It might also be useful. Both can be true.

A pentadecapeptide derived from a protective protein in human gastric juice. Animal data — and there is a lot of it — shows consistent effects on tendon healing, gut barrier integrity, and wound recovery. The mechanism is plausible. The model organisms are mostly rats.

Human data is where the gap appears. As of April 2026, we count nine registered human trials, six completed, three with published results. Two are open-label. None exceed N=80. None are double-blinded RCTs in healthy adults.

That isn't damning. It's where most peptides sit — somewhere between “the rats love it” and “your insurance will cover it.” The honest framing is promising-but-unproven, not basically aspirin.

What gets BPC-157 a B from us instead of a C: the animal evidence is unusually consistent across labs, dose ranges, and injury models. Replication is the entire point.

“ BPC-157 isn't slop. It also isn't aspirin. The honest grade is promising — and that's a useful word, if you let it mean what it means.
RECEIPTS
  1. [01] Sikiric et al. J Pharm Sci 2018 — tendon healing in rat Achilles, N=72
  2. [02] Chang et al. Eur J Pharm 2014 — anastomotic healing, rat colon
  3. [03] ClinicalTrials.gov NCT04938843 — human pilot, knee, completed 2023
  4. [04] ClinicalTrials.gov NCT05521022 — human IBD, recruiting 2026
