How to use BadgerSkope

BadgerSkope is a free, open peptide research library. Everything here is for education — not medical advice. Here's how to get the most out of it.

Browsing & Searching

Search bar
Type any word to search across titles, summaries, benefits, dosing notes, and more. Supports multiple words — all must match. An autocomplete dropdown shows matching titles as you type.
Filter dropdowns
Category: Filter by wellness area (weight, brain, immune, etc.). Type: Filter by compound class. Known for: Filter by research theme. Evidence: Show only entries at a specific evidence level. Sort: Reorder by name, evidence strength, or type.
Quick find buttons
The row of pill buttons below filters lets you jump to common goals like “Weight loss” or “Brain & mood” with one click.
Group by
Use the “Group by” dropdown to organize entries into labeled sections by theme, compound type, evidence tier, or category.
Category chips
Click any colored category chip on a card to instantly filter by that category. A “Filter” tooltip appears on hover.
Reset filters
A red “Reset filters” button appears whenever any filter is active. Click it to clear everything.

Reading an Entry

Opening details
Click any card to open its full detail view. You’ll see the compound name, type, and evidence level in a header bar.
Evidence badges
Color-coded badges tell you how strong the evidence is. Green = FDA approved. Teal = strong human trials. Amber = early human studies. Orange = animal studies only. Gray = clinic practice or unknown. Hover any badge for a full explanation.
Evidence in context
A chart shows how this entry’s evidence compares to others in the same category. Look for the verdict: “strongest,” “above average,” or “below average.”
Source quality
A quality bar above the sources shows what percentage are from high-quality origins (FDA labels and peer-reviewed papers score highest).
Unit tooltips
Hover over units like mg, mcg, IU, or terms like HbA1c, IGF-1, BDNF to see plain-English definitions.
Sport/doping flags
Compounds banned by WADA or flagged by anti-doping agencies show an amber warning badge on cards and a banner in the detail view.
Collapsible sections
“Cycling” and “Published doses” sections start collapsed. Click to expand them.

Selecting & Comparing

Selecting entries
Use the checkbox on each card, or click “Select all” to select everything visible. The selection count shows in the toolbar and on the Compare tab.
Compare tab
With 2+ entries selected, switch to the Compare tab to see them side by side. Differences are highlighted. “Best” badges mark the strongest evidence.
Stack synergy map
The Compare tab also shows a synergy map revealing which of your selected compounds are discussed together in research or product lines.
Export CSV
Click “Export CSV” in the selection toolbar to download your selected entries as a spreadsheet.
View details (multi)
Click “View details” with multiple selections to browse through them one by one with Previous/Next navigation.

Personal Tools

Bookmarks
Click the star on any card or in the detail view to bookmark it. Toggle “Bookmarked only” in the toolbar to see only your bookmarks. Press b to bookmark, f to filter.
Private notes
Each detail view has a “Your private notes” section at the bottom. Your notes are saved only in your browser — never sent anywhere.
Recently viewed
A bar above the grid shows your last 6 viewed entries for quick access. Click × to clear history.
Reading tracker
A small green dot appears on cards you’ve already opened, so you can track what you’ve read.
Share & print
Use “Copy link” in the detail view to share a direct link to an entry. Use “Print” for a clean paper copy.

Stats Dashboard

Overview
The Stats tab shows the database at a glance — total compounds and distribution charts for compound types, categories, evidence quality, and themes.
Click to filter
Click any bar in the Stats dashboard to switch to the Browse tab filtered by that value.

Other Features

Dark/light theme
Click the sun/moon icon or press t. Your preference is saved. On first visit, it matches your system setting.
Offline access
After your first visit, BadgerSkope works offline — the full database is cached in your browser.
Related & similar
“Often discussed alongside” shows synergy-linked compounds. “Similar compounds” suggests entries in the same categories.
Report an issue
Found an error? Click “Report issue” in the detail view to open a pre-filled GitHub issue.

BadgerSkope is a research literacy tool, not medical advice. All data is stored locally on your device. View on GitHub.
